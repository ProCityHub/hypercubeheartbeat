# LL-07 Word-Coordinate Rater Instructions

## Purpose

You will rate ordinary English words on three separate 0 to 100 scales.

This is a language-rating task.

It does not ask whether any belief, story, theory, or symbol is true.

Rate the word itself.

## Scales

### Observer scale

Question:

How much does this word feel related to perceiving, noticing, watching, witnessing, awareness, or receiving information?

0 means not related at all.

100 means strongly related.

### Actor scale

Question:

How much does this word feel related to doing, acting, changing, causing, choosing, moving, building, or exerting force?

0 means not related at all.

100 means strongly related.

### Bridge scale

Question:

How much does this word feel related to connecting, relating, joining, translating, carrying, mediating, linking, or integrating?

0 means not related at all.

100 means strongly related.

## Rating rule

Each scale is independent.

A word may be high on more than one scale.

A word may be low on all three scales.

Do not try to make the three numbers add to 100.

## Attention checks

The final rating form will include attention-check rows.

Attention-check rows are not part of the 100-word stimulus list.

A rater fails the attention check if the required values are missed by more than 5 points.

## Blinding

Raters should not be told the theory name, formula, hypothesis, or expected result.

Raters should only receive these instructions and the rating form.

## Output format

The rating file must contain:

- rater_id
- item_id
- word
- O
- A
- B

O, A, and B must be numeric values from 0 to 100.
